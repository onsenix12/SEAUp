# SEA-Up Creative — Consolidated User Journey
> Version 2 — Updated March 15, 2026
> Reflects: three learning journeys, Option A facilitator mode, production and selling phases.
> This is the single source of truth for the full user experience.

---

## The Three Actors

**ID Creator** — uses the app alone or with a facilitator. All creative decisions are theirs. The app is fully usable without any facilitator present.

**Facilitator** — disability org staff (e.g. MINDS Singapore). Not a SEA-Up employee. Uses the same device as the creator, seeing prompt cards between each step. Only present if the creator needs support — their involvement is optional, not required.

**Organizer** — SEA-Up team. Reviews marketplace submissions, refines Creation Stories, sets pricing, manages payouts. Currently manual (Avi + team).

---

## Two Modes of Use

### Mode 1 — Creator Alone
The ID creator opens the app directly. No login required. They tap through the full journey independently. No facilitator prompt cards appear. The flow is entirely self-directed.

### Mode 2 — Facilitated Session
The facilitator logs in via magic link → Session Setup screen → enters creator name and organisation → taps "Start Creation Flow." The device is now in facilitated mode. After each creator step, a facilitator prompt card appears on the same device before the next step loads. The facilitator reads the prompt, has the conversation, then taps "Continue" to hand the device back to the creator.

Both modes use the identical creation flow. The only difference is the presence or absence of facilitator prompt cards between steps.

## Phase 1 — Learning Phase

### Entry Point

The first screen the creator sees:

> **"What do you want to explore today?"**
> 😊 My Feelings · 🗺️ My World · 🎵 My Sounds

Three large visual cards. No text required to understand them. The creator taps one. This is their choice — not the facilitator's. The facilitator may verbally suggest a journey if the creator is unsure, but the app does not restrict the choice.

---

### Journey 1 — My Feelings

**Learning goal:** Emotional literacy. Identifying, naming, and contextualising an internal emotional state. Research basis: Zones of Regulation (Kuypers, 2011).

**Output:** A gallery-grade artwork that is a visual record of the creator's specific emotional experience — something they can show to family, keep as a personal artifact, or sell.

---

**Step 1 — What are you feeling today?**

*Creator sees:* 3 large illustrated emotion options. Pre-seeded, contextually selected by Gemini from a broader set. Examples: 😊 Happy · 😌 Calm · 😟 Worried · 🤩 Excited · 😢 Sad · 😕 Confused.

*Creator action:* Tap one.

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only, shown before this step):*
> "Ask them: How are you feeling right now? Point to the one that feels most like you."

---

**Step 2 — Where do you feel this?**

*Creator sees:* 3 context options generated by Gemini based on the chosen emotion.
Example for "Happy": 🏠 At home · 👫 With someone · 🌟 At a special place.

*Creator action:* Tap one.

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only):*
> "Ask them: Can you tell me a little about that place or person? What do you see there?"

---

**Step 3 — Pick your colours**

*Creator sees:* 3 colour palette swatches reflecting the emotional tone of their choices so far.
Example for "Happy + At home": Warm sunrise tones · Soft greens · Bright yellows.

*Creator action:* Tap one.

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only):*
> "Ask them: Which colours feel like how you're feeling right now?"

---

**Step 4 — Draw it**

*Creator sees:* Canvas opens with a gentle prompt:
> *"Draw what this feeling looks like. Use the stickers or draw freely."*

Sticker set: 😊 Face · ❤️ Heart · ☀️ Sun · 🌧️ Rain · 🏠 Home · 🌳 Tree · ⭐ Star.
Creator draws freely, places stickers, or both. No wrong answer. No time limit.

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only, shown before canvas opens):*
> "Ask them: Is there anything you want to add — a person, a place, a feeling? Let them draw as long as they want. Tap Continue when they feel done."

*Creator action:* Draws freely. Taps "I'm done" when ready.

---

**Step 5 — Generating**

Full-screen pixel-breath animation.

*In the backend:*
Gemini 2.5 Flash receives: emotion + context + colour palette + canvas spatial data. Constructs a high-fidelity prompt reflecting the specific emotional journey. Imagen 4 renders the artwork. SafeSearch runs automatically. Artwork appears within 15 seconds.

No facilitator card here — this step requires no human input.

---

**Step 6 — Your Feeling, Made Visible**

*Creator sees:* Full-screen artwork. Below it:
- Creation Story: *"Made while exploring [emotion] at [context]. Created on [date]."*
- Skills Practiced card: 😊 Emotional Expression · 🎨 Colour Association · ✨ Creative Choice

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only):*
> "Ask them: Does this look like how you were feeling? What do you see in it? Tell them: this is yours — you made this."

---

### Journey 2 — My World

**Learning goal:** Spatial reasoning and functional independence. Building a mental and visual map of a familiar environment. Research basis: Travel Training methodology (SPD, MINDS).

**Output:** A personalised neighbourhood artwork — the creator's actual environment rendered as a heritage print or abstract map.

---

**Step 1 — Where do you want to explore?**

*Creator sees:* 3 place options.
Examples: 🏘️ My neighbourhood · 🏫 My school · 💛 My favourite place.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Where do you go most days? Where do you feel comfortable?"

---

**Step 2 — What do you see there?**

*Creator sees:* 3 landmark category options generated by Gemini for the chosen place.
Example for "My neighbourhood": 🏢 Buildings · 🌳 Nature · 🚌 Transport.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: When you're there, what do you notice first? What stands out?"

---

**Step 3 — Pick your colours**

*Creator sees:* 3 palette options reflecting the environment's visual character.
Example for Transport: Steel blues and greys · Bright signal colours · Warm evening tones.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: What colour does that place feel like to you?"

---

**Step 4 — Draw your journey**

*Creator sees:* Canvas opens with a guided prompt:
> *"Let's draw your journey. Where do you start? Place your first sticker."*

Sticker set: 🏠 Home · 🚌 Bus Stop · 🚇 MRT · 🏫 School · 🌳 Park · 🏪 Shop · ⭐ Favourite Place.
Creator places stickers and draws a route connecting them. The spatial arrangement — sticker positions and drawn path — is captured and passed to Gemini as compositional data.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Where do you start from? Where do you go next? Help them build the route step by step. Tap Continue when the map feels complete."

*Creator action:* Places stickers, draws route. Taps "I'm done."

---

**Step 5 — Generating**

Gemini receives: place + landmark type + colour palette + canvas spatial data (sticker positions + drawn route). Renders as a stylised neighbourhood heritage print or abstract map — the creator's actual journey made beautiful. Imagen 4 generates. SafeSearch runs. Artwork within 15 seconds.

---

**Step 6 — Your World, Made Visible**

*Creator sees:* Full-screen artwork.
- Creation Story: *"A journey through [place]. Created on [date]."*
- Skills Practiced card: 🗺️ Spatial Reasoning · 📍 Wayfinding · ✨ Creative Choice

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Can you see your journey in the picture? Point to where home is. Tell them: you just drew your own map."

---

### Journey 3 — My Sounds

**Learning goal:** Auditory discrimination and environmental awareness. Identifying, sequencing, and contextualising sounds from the creator's environment. Research basis: Nordoff-Robbins music therapy model.

**Output:** A 48kHz ambient music track that reflects the creator's sonic world — licensable as a Lo-Fi track or environmental soundscape.

---

**Step 1 — Where are these sounds from?**

*Creator sees:* 3 environment options.
Examples: 🏘️ My neighbourhood · 🏠 My home · 💛 A place I love.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Close your eyes for a moment. What do you hear right now? Where does that sound come from?"

---

**Step 2 — Pick your sounds**

*Creator sees:* 6–8 sound chips for the chosen environment.
Example for My neighbourhood: 🐦 Birds · 🚌 Bus · 👣 Footsteps · 🌧️ Rain · 🛵 Scooter · 🎶 Music from a shop.
Creator taps 1–3 sounds.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Which sounds do you hear most? Which ones do you like?"

---

**Step 3 — What is the feeling of these sounds?**

*Creator sees:* 3 emotional tone options.
Examples: ⚡ Busy and energetic · 🌊 Calm and quiet · ☀️ Happy and warm.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Do these sounds make you feel calm or busy? Fast or slow?"

---

**Step 4 — Draw the rhythm**

*Creator sees:* Canvas opens with a prompt:
> *"Draw how the sounds move. Fast lines or slow? Big shapes or small?"*

No stickers for this journey — free drawing only. The drawing is used by Gemini to interpret rhythmic intent. Fast chaotic lines → energetic tempo. Slow sweeping lines → slower pace. The spatial data feeds directly into the music generation prompt.

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Ask them: Show me with your hand how the sound moves. Now draw it. Is it fast or slow?"

*Creator action:* Draws. Taps "I'm done."

---

**Step 5 — Generating**

Gemini receives: environment + sound selections + emotional tone + canvas rhythm data. Lyria 2 via Replicate composes a 48kHz ambient track. Waveform animation plays. Generation: 30–55 seconds.

---

**Step 6 — Your Sounds, Made Audible**

*Creator sees:* Music player with cover art (generated from canvas drawing).
- Creation Story: *"Composed from the sounds of [place]. Created on [date]."*
- Skills Practiced card: 🎵 Sound Discrimination · 🔢 Sequencing · ✨ Creative Choice

*Facilitator prompt card:*
> "Play it to them. Ask them: Does this sound like your place? What do you hear in it? Tell them: you made this music."

## Phase 2 — Production Phase

### The Decision Moment

After any journey completes, the creator sees their artwork or music with three options:

> **"What do you want to do with this?"**
> 🖨️ Print for myself · 🏪 Send to the shop · 💾 Save to my gallery

These are not mutually exclusive.

*Facilitator prompt card (Mode 2 only):*
> "Ask them: Do you want to keep a copy for yourself? Do you want other people to be able to buy it? They can do both."

---

### Path A — Print for Myself

Creator taps "Print for myself." Three product mockups appear: framed print · tote bag · phone case. Creator taps one.

If Mode 2: facilitator confirms the delivery address (pre-filled from organisation). Creator taps confirm.

Order sent to Printful (print-on-demand). Production and shipping handled entirely by POD provider. Creator receives a physical product with their artwork — a dignity moment.

---

### Path B — Send to the Shop

Creator taps "Send to the shop." Confirmation screen:
> *"Your artwork will be reviewed before it goes into the shop. We'll let you know when it's ready."*

Status set to `pending_review` in database.

**Facilitator review (Mode 2 — first gate):**
Facilitator receives notification in their dashboard. They confirm:
- Creator made this willingly
- Content appropriate for public display
- Creator name attribution is correct

Facilitator taps "Approve for organizer review." Status → `pending_organizer_review`.
If concern: "Return to creator" with note. Artwork stays in private gallery.

**Solo mode (no facilitator):**
Artwork goes directly to `pending_organizer_review`. Organizer applies additional care during review knowing no facilitator safeguarding step occurred.

---

### Path C — Save to Gallery

Creator taps "Save to my gallery." Artwork saved privately. Visible only to creator (and facilitator if Mode 2). Can be submitted to the shop later at any time from the gallery.

## Phase 3 — Selling Phase

### Organizer Review (Manual — SEA-Up Team)

Organizer opens `/admin/dashboard`. Reviews pending queue.

For each submission:
- Artwork or music track
- Creator first name and organisation
- Learning journey taken
- Skills Practiced tags
- AI-generated Creation Story draft
- Facilitator approval status (or "no facilitator" flag)

**Three tasks:**

**Task 1 — Quality and appropriateness check**
Is the output gallery-grade and appropriate for public commercial display? Fast check in most cases — SafeSearch + facilitator have already filtered. Organizer is checking commercial viability.

**Task 2 — Creation Story refinement**
The AI draft is a starting point. Organizer refines for emotional resonance and specificity.

Draft: *"Made while exploring happiness at home. Created on 14 March 2026."*
Refined: *"Painted in warm yellows and greens, this piece was created by Maya while exploring what happiness feels like at home. Made at MINDS Singapore, March 2026."*

**Task 3 — Set pricing**
Based on product type and format. Internal pricing guide:
- Prints: $25–80 SGD
- Tote bags: $35–55 SGD
- Digital licenses: $50–200 SGD
- Music tracks: $20–100 SGD

Organizer taps "Approve and list." Status → `approved`. Listing goes live immediately.

---

### Marketplace Display

Every listing shows:
- Artwork (full-screen) or music player with cover art
- Creator first name and organisation
- Creation Story (full text)
- Learning journey + Skills Practiced tags
- Product options and pricing
- *"This purchase supports [Name]'s creative journey"* badge

Corporate buyers additionally see:
- DE&I impact statement
- Bulk licensing enquiry option

---

### Purchase and Fulfilment

**B2C:** Buyer selects product → pays (Stripe/Xendit) → Printful produces and ships → digital downloads delivered immediately.

**B2B licensing:** Buyer enquires via marketplace form → SEA-Up issues licensing agreement → flat fee paid.

---

### Revenue Flow and Payout

All revenue → SEA-Up first. Monthly distribution at 60/20/20:

- **60% to creator** — paid to:
  - Guardian or care facility (if creator is a minor or lacks financial independence)
  - Creator directly (if they have their own bank account)
  - Payment destination nominated by facilitator's organisation during onboarding
- **20% to SEA-Up platform** — API costs, infrastructure, organizer time, development
- **20% to SEA-Up Community Fund** — onboards new creators, trains new facilitators, expands to new organisations

Creator receives a monthly visual earnings summary:
> *"This month, 3 people bought your artwork. You earned $45."*

Applies equally to artwork sales and music licensing.
